# **1\. Project Overview**

The Smart Medication Management System for Elderly Patients is a healthcare-focused web and mobile application that aims to help elderly individuals manage their daily medications safely and effectively. The application provides medication tracking, scheduled reminders, and a daily adherence dashboard through a simplified, Arabic-first interface.

This project was selected based on a structured evaluation of multiple ideas during Stage 1\. It achieved the highest score (28/30) and was unanimously chosen by the team for its strong real-world impact, alignment with team skills, and clear MVP scope.

 

# **2\. Project Objectives**

## **2.1 Purpose**

The purpose of this project is to reduce medication errors and improve treatment adherence among elderly patients by providing a centralized, user-friendly digital solution for managing prescriptions and medication schedules.

 

## **2.2 SMART Objectives**

The following SMART objectives guide the development and delivery of this project:

 

**Objective 1 – Medication Management Feature**

•       Specific: Develop a medication management module allowing users to add, view, and track medications with name, dosage, and schedule.

•        Measurable: User can add at least 5 medications with complete details (name, dosage,     schedule) in under 2 minutes.

•       Achievable: Implementable using standard database and frontend technologies within the team's skill set.

•       Relevant: Directly addresses the core problem of disorganized prescription tracking.

•       Time-bound: Completed and tested within the designated development phase.

 

**Objective 2 – Reminder Notification System**

•       Specific: Implement a scheduled notification system that alerts users when a medication is due or missed.

•       Measurable: Notification system successfully delivers 95% of scheduled reminders within 1 minute of the scheduled time during testing.

•       Achievable: Built using standard push notification frameworks available in the chosen tech stack.

•       Relevant: Directly targets the problem of forgotten medication doses.

•       Time-bound: Functional and tested by the end of the development phase.

 

**Objective 3 – Accessible User Interface**

•       Specific: Design and deliver an Arabic-first interface with large text, simple navigation, and high-contrast visuals optimized for elderly users.

•       Measurable: Interface passes basic usability review with large font sizes

•       Achievable: Achievable using standard UI frameworks and accessibility best practices.

•       Relevant: Ensures the application is usable by the primary target audience.

•       Time-bound: UI design finalized and implemented by the end of the development phase.

# **4\. Project Scope**

## **4.1 In-Scope**

The following features and activities are included in the MVP:

**User Interface:**

•       Arabic-first user interface optimized for elderly accessibility (large fonts, simple navigation).

•       Daily medication dashboard: overview of today's medications and adherence tracking.

**Technical Deliverables:**  

•       Frontend and backend development and integration.

•       Basic testing and quality assurance of core features.

•       Project documentation: charter, plan, and final report.

**Core Medication app:**

•       Reminder notification system: scheduled alerts for medication times and missed dose notifications.

•       Medication management: (add, view, edit, and delete) medications with name, dosage, and schedule.

•       Drug interaction detection.

•       User account management: registration and login.

•      In-app medication search.

•      Adherence rate calculation (taken / not taken).

•      Post-dose prompt asking user if taken.  

•      Add dependents (children, spouse, elderly parents).

•      Separate page for each dependent. 

•      Pill quantity tracking with low stock alert.

•       Family or caregiver monitoring dashboard.

•       Full tracking and analytics system for long-term missed medication patterns.

## **4.2 Out-of-Scope**

The following items are explicitly excluded from the current MVP:

•     Direct hospital & pharmacy integration: syncing prescriptions automatically from clinics or hospitals.

•    Wearable device integration: syncing with smartwatches to detect missed doses or health vitals.

•    Multi-language support: expanding beyond Arabic to support other languages.

•    Cloud-based medical history: storing and sharing full medical records across devices.

•    Emergency alert system: notifying family members automatically if a critical dose is missed repeatedly.

•    Medication cost tracking: helping users track spending on medications over time 

         

# **5\. Risk Management** 

## **5.1 Introduction**

Risk management is an essential part of project planning and development. Every project may face challenges that can affect timelines, functionality, team performance, or user satisfaction. Identifying risks early allows the team to prepare solutions before problems become critical.

For the **Smart Medication Management System for Patients**, the team conducted a risk analysis process to identify possible technical, organizational, and usability-related risks. Each risk was evaluated based on its potential impact on the project and the likelihood of occurring. Mitigation strategies were then developed to reduce risks and ensure smoother project execution.

The team focused on risks related to:

·       Leadership and project safety  
·       User trust and adoption  
·       Technology and development  
·       Team dynamics and collaboration  
·       Scope and time management

This proactive approach helps improve decision-making, reduce uncertainty, and increase the likelihood of delivering a successful MVP.

## **5.2 Leadership and Safety Risks**

Strong leadership and organized project management are important for maintaining project stability and ensuring team members stay aligned with deadlines and objectives.

One potential risk is the lack of clear leadership or delayed decision-making, which could slow project progress and create confusion within the team. To reduce this risk, the team assigned clear roles and responsibilities from the beginning of the project. Regular meetings and project tracking tools such as Trello and GitHub Projects are also used to maintain organization and accountability.

Another possible issue is team members missing deadlines or not completing assigned tasks on time. To address this, the team agreed to divide work into smaller tasks with clear timelines and monitor progress during weekly meetings.

## **5.3 Trust and User Adoption Risks**

Since the application targets regular and elderly users, trust and usability are critical factors for project success.

One major risk is that elderly users may find the application difficult to use or may not trust digital healthcare systems. To mitigate this risk, the team focused on designing a simple and accessible interface using large buttons, readable fonts, clear navigation, and an Arabic-first design approach.

Another challenge is the possibility that users may ignore medication reminder notifications. The team plans to minimize this risk by implementing clear and reliable notification systems with simple reminder messages that are easy for elderly users to understand.

The team also recognizes that healthcare-related applications require user confidence and simplicity. Therefore, unnecessary complexity will be avoided during the MVP phase.

## **5.4 Technology Risks**

Technology-related risks can significantly affect development progress and system reliability.

One identified risk is the possibility of notification systems failing or becoming unreliable. Since reminder functionality is a core feature of the MVP, the team plans to prioritize testing and stability during development.

Another challenge is limited experience with certain tools or technologies required for development. To reduce this risk, the team agreed to allocate time for learning, share tutorials, and encourage collaborative problem-solving between team members.

The project may also face technical complexity if healthcare integrations become too advanced during early stages. To avoid unnecessary development risks, the MVP scope excludes direct hospital or pharmacy integrations.

Continuous testing and phased development will help the team detect and resolve technical issues early.

## **5.5 Team Dynamics Risks**

Successful collaboration is essential for completing the project efficiently.

One possible risk is miscommunication between team members, especially during task coordination or technical discussions. To reduce this issue, the team established clear communication channels using Discord and WhatsApp and scheduled regular meetings to review progress.

Another risk involves unequal workload distribution, where some team members may become overloaded while others contribute less. The team plans to prevent this by assigning clear responsibilities and reviewing progress collectively.

Conflicts during discussions or decision-making may also occur. To address this, the team agreed to use collaborative discussions and evidence-based reasoning when resolving disagreements.

Maintaining respectful communication and equal participation remains one of the team’s core working principles.

## **5.6 Scope and Time Management Risks**

Projects often face risks related to expanding requirements and limited development time.

One identified risk is scope expansion beyond MVP requirements. Adding too many advanced features could delay development and reduce overall project quality. To manage this risk, the team clearly defined in-scope and out-of-scope features at the beginning of the project.

Another challenge is limited time for completing all planned tasks. The team plans to focus only on essential MVP functionality during early stages and postpone advanced features for future development phases.

A structured timeline and milestone-based planning approach will help maintain progress and ensure realistic workload management.

## **5.7 External Market and User Risks**

External risks are factors outside the direct control of the development team that can still significantly affect project success, user adoption, and long term viability.   
These risks relate to market competition, user technology literacy, and broader environmental factors.  
One major external risk is the presence of established competitors in the medication reminder space. Existing applications such as Medisafe, MyTherapy, Pill Reminder, and CareClinic already have larger user bases, more advanced features, and better funding. This makes it difficult for a new application to attract and retain users. To mitigate this risk, the team will differentiate the application through dependent management features and a first Arabic design. The focus will remain on simplicity and user trust rather than matching competitors on feature quantity. A competitor analysis will also be conducted to identify specific gaps in existing solutions, such as the lack of comprehensive family caregiver views.

Another significant external risk is that elderly users may lack basic technology literacy.   
Many elderly patients do not know how to use smartphones, install mobile applications, or manage notification settings. This can lead to complete non-adoption of the application regardless of its quality. To reduce this risk, the team will provide a simple onboarding tutorial using images and large buttons. A printed user guide will be made available for family members to assist with setup. A video tutorial accessible directly from the home screen will also be considered. Additionally, the application will support family assisted setup, where a dependent family member can install and configure the app for the elderly user. 

Low digital trust among elderly users presents another challenge. Fear of data privacy breaches, online scams, or accidental charges may prevent elderly users from trusting or using the application. To address this, the team will emphasize that the MVP is completely free with no payment required. Local data storage options will be offered where possible, and a clear privacy policy written in simple Arabic will be provided. Familiar branding will be used, and any appearance of financial transactions will be avoided entirely. 

Family members or caregivers may prefer existing solutions over a new application. Many adult children already use WhatsApp or simple phone alarms to remind their parents about medications, which reduces their motivation to switch to a dedicated app. To address this risk, the team will clearly highlight the added value over basic reminders, including adherence tracking, missed dose reports, multi medication scheduling, and journaling features. An easy invite link will allow family members to join the platform with minimal friction.
